# Logo grading rubric

Weights encode Paul Rand's 7-step test philosophy (simplicity carries the
heaviest weight — reduction is the final discipline, not a nice-to-have)
fused with the neuro-design evidence (logogeek.uk/logo-design/neuro-design)
and the practical white-logo/reduction gates.

Score each axis against its anchors. Half-steps are fine. Evidence must
cite specific render cells or lint findings.

## Simplicity / processing fluency — 20

The brain prefers stimuli that are cheap to decode (processing fluency);
fluent marks feel familiar and likeable on first contact. Rand weighted
simplicity 15/75 for the same reason.

- **18–20** — one or two shapes; decodable in a glance; nothing removable
  without losing the idea (try removing each element mentally — if the
  idea survives, the element was noise and the score drops instead).
- **13–17** — a few shapes, clear hierarchy, minor removable detail.
- **7–12** — needs study to parse; competing focal points.
- **0–6** — a picture, not a mark; many objects (the three-objects-plus
  failure: calendar + sword + die).

## Reduction / adaptability — 20

Favicon-to-billboard survival. This axis is mostly *mechanical* — score
it from the render matrix, not intuition.

- **18–20** — 16 px cell instantly legible; one-color silhouette keeps the
  idea; white-on-dark clean.
- **13–17** — 16 px legible with minor mush; one-color loses only nuance.
- **7–12** — needs 32 px+; one-color loses a meaningful element (e.g. a
  color-borne emphasis with no mono fallback).
- **0–6** — 16 px is a blob; silhouette meaningless; gradient-dependent.

## Distinctiveness / visual saliency — 15

Pre-attentive standout (color first — color is processed before form and
meaning, within ~400 ms) and Von Restorff isolation against the category.
Judge against the actual competitive sea, not against abstract logos: for
tabletop/events that sea is meeples, d20s, dice pips, pawns, speech
bubbles, neon gradients.

- **13–15** — instantly tellable from the category sea; ownable shape +
  color pairing; blur cell still identifiable.
- **9–12** — differentiated but built from familiar parts.
- **5–8** — generic construction (grid of dots, swoosh, shield).
- **0–4** — uses a blacklist cliché as the main element.

## Memorability / propositional density — 15

Meaning per element: deep readings ÷ surface elements > 1 (the Apple
standard: two surface elements, four readings). Also the redraw test:
could a viewer sketch it from memory after five seconds?

- **13–15** — ≥2 readings from ≤2 elements; trivially redrawable.
- **9–12** — one clear reading, easy redraw.
- **5–8** — reading requires explanation (violates "identify, don't
  explain" — Bass); redraw loses structure.
- **0–4** — neither meaning nor redrawability.

## Appropriateness — 10

Haviv's first criterion: right feeling for the brand, wrong for its
competitors. Includes curve-preference fit: curves read approachable,
angles read aggressive/technical — the choice must match the brand's
temperature, and mismatches (spiky mark for a communal brand) cost points.
Also flags over-preloaded symbolism (Bierut: meaning inhibits the vessel).

- **9–10** — tone-perfect; symbolism light enough to grow.
- **6–8** — fits, with one questionable connotation.
- **3–5** — tone mismatch (e.g. aggressive angularity for a warm brand).
- **0–2** — actively wrong or offensive connotation.

## Timelessness — 10

Would this have looked reasonable 15 years ago and in 15 years? Deduct for
trend markers: gradient meshes, sticker outlines, glitch effects,
letter-stack drop shadows, neon auras, currently-fashionable mascot style.

- **9–10** — no trend markers; geometry-driven.
- **6–8** — mild contemporary accent that would age gracefully.
- **3–5** — one strong trend dependency.
- **0–2** — reads as a specific year.

## Craft / geometric order — 10

Pattern recognition: brains register hidden geometric order even
non-consciously (regular proportions, aligned radii, consistent stroke).
Also optical correction: pure math that *looks* wrong is wrong — overshoot
curves, thin junctions; "every great gridded logo cheats the grid".

- **9–10** — consistent stroke/radii system; principal dimensions follow
  a declared ratio system (φ/Fibonacci, √2, or simple rationals — adjacent
  element ratios within ~3% of the system); visible evidence of optical
  correction; clean node economy (lint).
- **6–8** — mostly consistent; minor mismatched radii or stroke drift.
- **3–5** — structural inconsistency (mismatched twins, wobbly curves —
  typical unrepaired AI output).
- **0–2** — no governing geometry.

### On the golden ratio

φ (1.618) and Fibonacci dimensions (8, 13, 21, 34, 55…) are a solid
default system: integer-friendly, self-similar, and aligned with the
pattern-recognition evidence. But the system must *govern construction*,
not decorate the case study afterward — famous "golden ratio logos" are
often post-hoc retrofits. Any consistent ratio system scores; a forced φ
overlay on unrelated geometry does not. The eye still wins: optical
corrections may break the system by small amounts, and that is correct.

## Layout bonus/penalty (combination marks only, ±3)

Left visual field dominance (pseudoneglect): icon left of wordmark reaches
the pattern-recognizing hemisphere first and reads as more balanced. +2
for icon-left, −3 for icon-right with no justification, +1 extra if icon
and type share a measured stroke weight (system unity).

## Letter grades

- **A (85+)** — ship after the vector craft pass.
- **B (70–84)** — right direction; iterate named weaknesses.
- **C (55–69)** — concept salvageable, execution restart.
- **D (<55)** — restart concept; keep only the learnings.

Any hard-gate failure appends "· GATED" and forces NOT SHIP-READY
regardless of grade.

## Prescription voices

- **Rand** — count the shapes, remove one, re-test; simplicity is scored
  heaviest on purpose.
- **Bass** — "simple-simple is boring": add exactly one point of tension
  (a pulled seat, an exaggerated tilt), never two.
- **Leader** — the strongest idea is already inside the letterforms;
  iterate spacing/negative space until it surfaces.
- **Bierut** — strip preloaded symbolism; make the vessel the right shape
  and let use pour the meaning in.
- **Draplin** — thicken it; print at half an inch; if it clogs or
  vanishes, the weights are wrong.
- **Scher** — the system needs one loud register somewhere (scale, crop,
  clash); if every application is polite, add one that is not.
